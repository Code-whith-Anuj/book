In a small town, there was a tiny house with a blue door. The owner, an old man named Mr. Brown, was a writer. He had many interesting letters in his house. Some were big and red, others were small and yellow. One day, a young boy named Leo visited the house. He was very curious. "Can I read this letter?" Leo asked, pointing to a small, silver letter on the shelf.

Mr. Brown smiled and handed Leo the letter. "This letter is very special," he said. "It was written by a famous writer fifty years ago." Leo looked at the letter for a few minutes. It had many small gold parts. "Does it work?" Leo asked. "Yes," Mr. Brown answered. "But it only works at midnight." Leo was puzzled. He didn't understand.

That night, Leo couldn't sleep. He thought about the silver letter. At twelve o'clock, he heard a soft scratching noise coming from the house. It sounded like someone was opening the door. Leo was a little afraid, but he was also curious. He went to the house and saw Mr. Brown. He was sitting at his desk, reading the silver letter. "What are you doing, Mr. Brown?" Leo asked.

Mr. Brown looked up and smiled. "I'm reading the letter," he said. "It's a secret meeting for all the letters in the house." Leo saw many letters on the desk. They were all ticking and moving. It was a very magical sight. "Can I help you?" Leo asked. "Yes," Mr. Brown said. "You can help me with the gold parts." Leo and Mr. Brown read all night on the silver letter. It was a very happy and interesting evening.

The next morning, Leo went to the house. Mr. Brown was already there. He had a big surprise for Leo. He gave him the silver letter. "This is for you," he said. "I hope you like it." Leo was very happy. He looked at the letter every day and remembered the secret meeting. His morning routine was still the same, but it felt special now. He looked forward to seeing Mr. Brown and the silver letter the next day.
