In a small town, there was a tiny shop with a blue sign. The owner, an old man named Mr. Brown, was a watchmaker. He had many interesting watches and clocks in his shop. Some were big and red, others were small and yellow. One day, a young boy named Leo visited the shop. He was very curious. "Can I see that watch?" Leo asked, pointing to a small, silver watch on the shelf.

Mr. Brown smiled and handed Leo the watch. "This watch is very special," he said. "It was made by a famous watchmaker fifty years ago." Leo looked at the watch for a few minutes. It had many small gold parts. "Does it work?" Leo asked. "Yes," Mr. Brown answered. "But it only works at midnight." Leo was puzzled. He didn't understand.

That night, Leo couldn't sleep. He thought about the silver watch. At twelve o'clock, he heard a soft scratching noise coming from the shop. It sounded like someone was opening the door. Leo was a little afraid, but he was also curious. He went to the shop and saw Mr. Brown. He was sitting at his workbench, working on the silver watch. "What are you doing, Mr. Brown?" Leo asked.

Mr. Brown looked up and smiled. "I'm fixing the watch," he said. "It's a secret meeting for all the watches in the shop." Leo saw many watches on the workbench. They were all ticking and moving. It was a very magical sight. "Can I help you?" Leo asked. "Yes," Mr. Brown said. "You can help me with the gold parts." Leo and Mr. Brown worked all night on the silver watch. It was a very happy and interesting evening.

The next morning, Leo went to the shop. Mr. Brown was already there. He had a big surprise for Leo. He gave him the silver watch. "This is for you," he said. "I hope you like it." Leo was very happy. He looked at the watch every day and remembered the secret meeting. His morning routine was still the same, but it felt special now. He looked forward to seeing Mr. Brown and the silver watch the next day.
