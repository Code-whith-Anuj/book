In a small town, there was a tiny house with a blue door. The owner, an old man named Mr. Brown, was a teacher. He had many interesting books in his house. Some were big and red, others were small and yellow. One day, a young girl named Lily visited the house. She was very curious. "Can I read this book?" Lily asked, pointing to a small, silver book on the shelf.

Mr. Brown smiled and handed Lily the book. "This book is very special," he said. "It was written by a famous author fifty years ago." Lily looked at the book for a few minutes. It had many small gold parts. "Does it work?" Lily asked. "Yes," Mr. Brown answered. "But it only works at midnight." Lily was puzzled. She didn't understand.

That night, Lily couldn't sleep. She thought about the silver book. At twelve o'clock, she heard a soft scratching noise coming from the house. It sounded like someone was opening the door. Lily was a little afraid, but she was also curious. She went to the house and saw Mr. Brown. He was sitting at his desk, reading the silver book. "What are you doing, Mr. Brown?" Lily asked.

Mr. Brown looked up and smiled. "I'm reading the book," he said. "It's a secret meeting for all the books in the house." Lily saw many books on the desk. They were all ticking and moving. It was a very magical sight. "Can I help you?" Lily asked. "Yes," Mr. Brown said. "You can help me with the gold parts." Lily and Mr. Brown read all night on the silver book. It was a very happy and interesting evening.

The next morning, Lily went to the house. Mr. Brown was already there. He had a big surprise for Lily. He gave her the silver book. "This is for you," he said. "I hope you like it." Lily was very happy. She looked at the book every day and remembered the secret meeting. Her morning routine was still the same, but it felt special now. She looked forward to seeing Mr. Brown and the silver book the next day.
