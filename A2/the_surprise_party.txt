Last week, it was Sarah's birthday. Her friends, Paul and Linda, wanted to plan a surprise party for her. They talked about the party for many days. They decided to have the party at Paul's house on Saturday evening.

On Saturday morning, Paul went to the supermarket. He bought many things: a large chocolate cake, some oranges, and small snacks. Linda went to the flower shop. She bought red and yellow flowers. She also bought some balloons. They worked all day to make the house look beautiful.

Paul called Sarah at five o'clock. "Can you come to my house at six?" he asked. "I have a new book for you." Sarah said, "Yes, I can come." She didn't know about the party. She was very surprised when she opened the door. All her friends shouted, "Surprise! Happy Birthday, Sarah!"

They ate the delicious cake and listened to music. Sarah liked the flowers very much. Everyone gave her small gifts. Paul gave her a new notebook, and Linda gave her a blue pen. Sarah felt very happy. "Thank you so much," she said to her friends. "This is the best birthday party ever." They stayed at the house until ten o'clock. It was a wonderful evening.
