Every Saturday morning, Maria and her mother went to the market. It was a large, noisy square in the center of the town. There were many stalls with fresh fruits and vegetables. The market was a very colorful place. Maria liked the smell of fresh bread and the sound of people talking.

Maria and her mother arrived at eight o'clock. "The early bird catches the worm!" her mother said. They visited the vegetable stall. "Good morning, Mrs. Green. Two kilos of potatoes and one kilo of carrots, please," Maria's mother said. Mrs. Green was a kind woman. She gave Maria a small red apple. "This is for you, Maria," she said.

Next, they went to the fruit stall. There were many oranges, bananas, and grapes. Maria liked the big, sweet oranges. They bought one kilo of oranges and a small basket of strawberries. The strawberries were very red and fresh. Maria's mother also bought a large brown loaf of bread from the baker.

When they finished, they went to a small cafe near the market. They sat at a small table and had two glasses of orange juice. They watched the people in the market. It was a busy morning. They carried their heavy bags home at ten o'clock. Maria's mother said, "We have many delicious things for the week." Maria was happy. She looked forward to the next Saturday morning at the market.
