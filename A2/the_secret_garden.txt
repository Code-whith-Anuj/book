Last month, Maria moved to a new house. The house was small and had a blue door. The street was quiet and full of green trees. Maria liked the new neighborhood, but she was a little lonely. She didn't know anyone yet.

On Friday morning, Maria went for a walk in her new street. She saw an old, tall gate at the end of the road. The gate was made of dark iron and it was half open. Maria was curious. She walked slowly through the gate and found herself in a large, secret garden. There were many big trees and colorful flowers. The garden was very quiet and peaceful.

"Whose garden is this?" Maria asked herself. She sat on a small stone bench and looked at the blue sky. She felt very happy. Then, she saw an old man sitting under a big tree. He was reading a book. He looked up and smiled at Maria. "Good morning, I'm Mr. Brown. I've lived in this house for forty years," he said.

Maria and Mr. Brown talked for ten minutes. Mr. Brown was a retired gardener. He loved the secret garden. He told Maria about all the different flowers and trees. Maria learned many things about gardening. "You can come here every day," Mr. Brown said. Maria was very happy. She had a new friend and a secret place to visit. She didn't feel lonely anymore. She looked forward to seeing Mr. Brown and the secret garden the next day.
