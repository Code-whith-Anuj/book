Every morning, David went to the same coffee shop. It was a small shop on a quiet street. It had big glass windows and three wooden tables. The smell of coffee was always there. David liked the small shop because it was peaceful. He liked his morning routine.

At eight o'clock, the door opened. A woman named Maria worked at the counter. She smiled at David. "Good morning, David. The usual?" she asked. David nod his head. He always ordered a large black coffee and a small piece of bread. He sat by the window. He looked at the people walking in the street.

One Tuesday morning, something was different. The coffee shop was busier than usual. There were many people and no empty tables. David stood near the door. He felt a bit lost. Then, an old man at the window table spoke to him. "You can sit here," the man said. David thanked him and sat down.

The old man's name was Mr. Black. He was an artist. He had a small sketchbook on the table. He was drawing the trees in the park across the street. David watched him for a few minutes. The drawing was very good. "Did you draw that today?" David asked. Mr. Black smiled. "Yes, I come here every morning too," he answered.

They talked for twenty minutes. They talked about the weather, the park, and the coffee. David learned that Mr. Black lived in the blue house at the end of the street. David told him that he worked in the library. It was a nice conversation. David felt happy that morning.

When it was time to leave, Mr. Black stood up. He took a pen and wrote something on a piece of paper. He gave it to David. "This is for you," he said. David looked at the paper. It was a small drawing of David's coffee cup on the wooden table. It was beautiful.

David went to work with a big smile. He put the drawing on his desk. Every time he looked at it, he remembered the kind artist. His morning routine was still the same, but it felt special now. He looked forward to seeing his new friend at the small coffee shop the next day.
